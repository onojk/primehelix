\noindent The Editors\\
\textit{Integers: Electronic Journal of Combinatorial Number Theory}

\bigskip

Dear Editors,

We study the coordinate \(\theta = \log p / \log n\) for semiprimes \(n = pq\)
(\(p \leq q\)), where \(\theta\) encodes the relative size of the smaller prime
factor in a scale-invariant way.  Our main results are: (1) the proportion
\(P(N, \theta_0)\) of semiprimes with \(\theta \leq \theta_0\) converges
extremely slowly --- consistent with \(O(1/\!\log\log N)\) decay --- documented
by exact enumeration to \(N = 10^8\); (2) discrete prime activation thresholds
at \(N = p^{1/\theta_0}\) produce step-like jumps in \(P(N, \theta_0)\), a
novel structural feature not captured by smooth asymptotic models; (3) a
second-order heuristic prefactor \(f(\theta_0) = \log(1/\theta_0) - \theta_0 -
\log 2 + \tfrac{1}{2}\) reduces the naive Mertens prediction error from
\(4.3\times\) to under \(1.21\times\) across \(\theta_0 \in [0.10, 0.45]\);
and (4) several natural geometric and spectral embeddings (additive phase
wrapping, multiplicative phase maps, spectral fingerprint, factor-ratio
spectrum) all fail to recover the structure carried by \(\theta\), consistent
with a separation principle.  All results are independently verified by exact
smallest-prime-factor sieve enumeration, with code and data available at
\url{https://github.com/onojk/primehelix}.

The paper is empirical and combinatorial in character, self-contained, and
computationally grounded with theoretical anchoring via Montgomery--Vaughan
(\S7.4) and Mertens' theorem.  The activation threshold observation and the
refined prefactor conjecture are novel contributions directly accessible to the
readership of \textit{Integers}.  The paper is approximately 14 pages including
appendices.

This paper is not under review elsewhere and has not been previously published.
All data and code are publicly available at the repository linked above.  The
author consents to open-access publication under the journal's standard terms.
